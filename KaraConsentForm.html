Key information: You are being asked to participate in a voluntary research study conducted by Dr. Kara Federmeier of the Department of Psychology at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign. The purpose of this study is to investigate the processes that people go through in order to make sense of words. Participating in this study will involve a combination of viewing words and responding to them with your computer keyboard and then filling out a questionnaire asking some basic information about you.

This study will take approximately 15~20 minutes of your time. You will receive $2.00 for your participation.

There are no anticipated risks, beyond those encountered in daily life, associated with participating in this study; there are also no direct benefits to you other than the payment. The alternative to participating in this study is to seek other opportunities for hourly payment.

Voluntary Withdrawal: Your decision to participate or decline participation in this study is completely voluntary and you have the right to terminate your participation at any time without penalty. If you do not wish to complete this survey, just close your browser.

Will my study-related information be kept confidential?

The only information about you that we will record is your Worker ID and your age range. Laws and university rules require us to inform you that in certain situations we may need to disclose your information. For example, if required by laws or University Policy, study information, including your Worker ID or age range, may be seen by the following people or groups:

• The university committee and office that reviews and approves research studies, the Institutional Review Board (IRB) and Office for Protection of Research Subjects;

• University and state auditors, and Departments of the university responsible for oversight of research;

• Federal government regulatory agencies such as the Office of Human Research Protections in the Department of Health and Human Services;

• Financial sponsors of the research, including Sandia National Laboratories.

Research Records: Data will be averaged and reported in aggregate. Possible outlets of dissemination include academic conferences and journals. Your de-identified information and data could be used for future research without additional informed consent.

If you have questions about this project, you may contact our lab at illinoiscablabmanager@gmail.com. If you have any questions about your rights as a research participant in the study, please contact the University of Illinois Institutional Review Board at 217-333-2670 (collect calls accepted if you identify yourself as a research participant) or via email at irb@illinois.edu.
