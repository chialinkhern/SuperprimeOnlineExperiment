Key information: An associate of the Department of Psychology at the University of Illinois is conducting this research. You are being asked to participate in a voluntary research study. The purpose of this study is to investigate the processes that people go through in order to make sense of words, sentences, and everyday objects. As described in more detail below, participating in this study will involve some combination of viewing or listening to everyday stimuli and responding to them. You may be asked comprehension questions about what you've seen or heard, to remember what you saw or heard earlier in the experiment, or to make judgments about how some stimuli fit together. You may also take some tests of knowledge, language, memory, attention, or response speed. Finally, you may be asked to complete questionnaires covering basic health, mental health, and personality characteristics. There are no anticipated risks, beyond those encountered in daily life, associated with participating in this study.

What will I do in this study? In this study, you will be asked to answer some questions about English words as quickly and accurately as possible. You will also be asked about your language experiences at the end of the study.

Participation and Voluntary Withdrawal: You must be 18 or older to participate. Participation is voluntary. You may quit the experiment at any point of the process by simply closing the window.

Risks and Research Records: There are no anticipated risks, beyond those encountered in daily life, associated with participating in this study. There are also no direct benefits to you other than payment. The alternative to participating in this study is to seek other opportunities for hourly payment. By participating in this study, you will help to contribute to the advancement of cognitive psychological research. Results of this study may be presented at conferences and/or published in books, journals, and/or in the popular media. Your de-identified information and data could be used for future research without additional informed consent.

Will my study-related information be kept confidential? Yes, but not always. In general, we will not tell anyone any information about you. Your responses will be assigned a code number that is not linked to any identifying information. When this research is discussed or published, no one will know that you were in the study. However, laws and university rules might require us to disclose information about you. For example, if required by laws or University Policy, study information may be seen by the following people or groups:

• The university committee and office that reviews and approves research studies, The Institutional Review Board (IRB) and Office for Protection of Research Subjects,

• University and state auditors, and Departments of the university responsible for oversight of research,

• Federal government regulatory agencies such as the Office of Human Research Protections in the Department of Health and Human Services,

• Financial sponsors of the research, including the National Institutes of Health.

Compensation: The study will take approximately 20 minutes. You will receive $2.00 for your participation. To prevent payment to bots and bad internet actors, we have set a performance threshold that will be easy to meet if you are engaged in the task. Your payment is contingent upon meeting this reasonable threshold that we have set.

Further Information: For further information about the experiment, you may contact us at lachia2@illinois.edu. If you have questions or concerns about your rights as a participant in the experiment, or if you would like information about the rights of research participants, please contact the University of Illinois Institutional Review Board (217-333-2670, irb@illinois.edu).
